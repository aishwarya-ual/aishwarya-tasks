Radhe Krishna राधे कृष्ण

srita-kamala-kuca-mandala dhruta-kundala e kalita-lalita-vana-mala jaya jaya deva hare ||dhruvapada||

Radha and Krishna are central figures in Hinduism, symbolizing the divine love and devotion between a devotee and the Supreme. Radha, often depicted as a gopi (milkmaid), is considered the beloved consort of Krishna.

vasante vasanti-kusuma-sukumarair avayavair bhramanti kantare bahu-vihita-krishnanusaraam | amanda kandarpa-jvara-janita-cintakulataya valad-baha radha sarasam idam uce saha-cari

Their love story, expressed through poetry and art, especially in Bengali literature, represents the mystical union of the human soul (Radha) with the divine (Krishna).

unmada-madana-manoratha-pathika-vadhu-jana-janita-vilape | ali-kula-sakula-kusuma-samuha-nirakula-bakula-kalape, Radhe viharati haririha ||2||

This divine love is a significant theme in the bhakti (devotional) movement within Vaishnavism, illustrating the soul's yearning for spiritual union with the divine.

mriga-mada-saurabha, rabhasa-vashavada-nava-dala-mala-tamale | yuva-jana-hrudaya-vidarana manasija, nakha-ruci-kinshuka-jale, Radhe viharati haririha ||3||

The Gita Govinda, a famous work by Jayadeva, and various artistic representations celebrate the eternal bond between Radha and Krishna in Hindu culture.

madana-mahipati-kanaka-danda-ruci, kesara-kusuma-vikase | milita-shili-mukha-paatala-patala,kruta-smara-tuna-vilase, Radhe viharati haririha ||4||
